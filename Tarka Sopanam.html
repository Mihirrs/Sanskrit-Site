5 MARKS

लघुप्रबन्धाः — Short Essays

Q19

अनुमितिः।

Write a short note on Anumiti (inferential cognition).

Answer — Sanskrit

अनुमितिः अनुमानजन्यं ज्ञानम् इति उच्यते। यत्र परामर्शात् साध्यविषयकं ज्ञानम् उत्पद्यते तत् अनुमितिः।

परामर्शः व्याप्तिविशिष्टपक्षधर्मताज्ञानम्। स एव अनुमितेः जनकः। यथा — पर्वते धूमं दृष्ट्वा "पर्वतो वह्निमान् धूमात्" इति ज्ञानम् अनुमितिः।

अनुमितिः स्वार्थानुमाने परार्थानुमाने च भिद्यते। स्वार्थानुमाने स्वज्ञानार्थम् अनुमानम्, परार्थानुमाने परस्मै बोधनार्थं पञ्चावयवीप्रक्रिया अपेक्षिता।

Answer — English

Anumiti is the cognition (jñāna) produced by anumāna (inference). It arises from parāmarśa — the cognition of the probans (hetu) as located in the pakṣa and pervaded by vyāpti.

Example: Seeing smoke on a mountain and knowing "the mountain has fire, because of smoke" — that cognition of fire is anumiti. The probans "smoke" (hetu) is pervaded by fire (vyāpti) and is present in the mountain (pakṣa); the resulting knowledge of fire there is the anumiti.

Anumiti is the goal of both svārthānumāna (inference for oneself) and parārthānumāna (inference presented to another). In the latter, it is formally expressed through the five-membered syllogism (pañcāvayavī).

Q20

याप्तिः।

Write a short note on Vyāpti (invariable concomitance).

Answer — Sanskrit

अव्यभिचरितसहचरणं व्याप्तिः। हेतोः साध्येन अनिवार्यसम्बन्धः व्याप्तिः इत्युच्यते।

व्याप्तिः द्विविधा — अन्वयव्याप्तिः विपक्षव्याप्तिः च। "यत्र धूमः तत्र वह्निः" इति अन्वयव्याप्तिः। "यत्र वह्निः नास्ति तत्र धूमः नास्ति" इति व्यतिरेकव्याप्तिः।

व्याप्तिग्रहणस्य उपायाः — भूयोदर्शनम्, उपाधिः, तर्कः च। व्याप्तिज्ञानम् अनुमानस्य मूलभूतं साधनम्।

Answer — English

Vyāpti is the invariable concomitance between hetu (probans) and sādhya (probandum) — the unfailing accompaniment of one with the other.

It has two forms: anvaya-vyāpti (positive) — "wherever there is smoke, there is fire" — and vyatireka-vyāpti (negative) — "wherever there is no fire, there is no smoke."

The means of cognising vyāpti are repeated observation (bhūyadarśana), elimination of upādhi (conditional qualifiers), and tarka (reductio ad absurdum reasoning). Vyāpti-jñāna is the foundation of all valid inference.

Q21

परामर्शलक्षणं सदलकृत्वं निरूपयत।

Explain the definition of Parāmarśa with illustration.

Answer — Sanskrit

व्याप्तिविशिष्टपक्षधर्मताज्ञानं परामर्शः। अर्थात् — पक्षे विद्यमानः हेतुः व्याप्तिसहितः इति यत् ज्ञानम् उत्पद्यते तत् परामर्शः।

यथा — "पर्वतः धूमवान्" इति पक्षधर्मताज्ञानम्। "धूमो वह्निव्याप्तः" इति व्याप्तिज्ञानम्। एतयोः संयोगेन "पर्वते धूमः वह्निव्याप्तः" इति यत् ज्ञानम् उत्पद्यते तत् परामर्शः।

परामर्शात् एव "पर्वतो वह्निमान्" इति अनुमितिः उत्पद्यते। अतः परामर्शः अनुमितेः साक्षात्कारणम् इति उच्यते।

Answer — English

Parāmarśa is defined as the cognition of the hetu as being present in the pakṣa and as being pervaded by vyāpti (vyāpti-viśiṣṭa-pakṣa-dharmatā-jñānam).

Example: "The mountain has smoke" is the pakṣa-dharmatā cognition. "Smoke is pervaded by fire" is the vyāpti cognition. The combined cognition — "the smoke that is on the mountain is pervaded by fire" — is parāmarśa.

From this parāmarśa directly arises the anumiti: "the mountain has fire." Thus parāmarśa is the immediate (sākṣāt) cause of the inferential cognition.

Q22

लिङ्गं त्रिविधम् — व्याख्यात।

The liṅga (mark/hetu) is of three kinds — Explain.

Answer — Sanskrit

लिङ्गं (हेतुः) त्रिविधम् — केवलान्वयी, केवलव्यतिरेकी, अन्वयव्यतिरेकी च।

१. केवलान्वयी — यः हेतुः केवलम् अन्वयव्याप्तिमान्, न तु व्यतिरेकव्याप्तिमान्। यथा — "इदं वक्तव्यम् अभिधेयत्वात्।" अत्र अभिधेयत्वस्य केवलान्वयव्याप्तिः अस्ति।

२. केवलव्यतिरेकी — यः हेतुः केवलम् व्यतिरेकव्याप्तिमान्। यथा — "आत्मा ज्ञानाश्रयः न, अज्ञानाश्रयत्वात्।"

३. अन्वयव्यतिरेकी — यः उभाभ्यां व्याप्तिभ्यां युक्तः। यथा — "पर्वतो वह्निमान् धूमात्।"

Answer — English

The liṅga (inferential mark / hetu) is of three kinds: Kevalānvayī, Kevalavyatirekī, and Anvaya-vyatirekī.

1. Kevalānvayī — has only positive (anvaya) vyāpti, no negative counterpart (because sādhya-abhāva has no known locus). E.g., "This is nameable, because it is expressible."

2. Kevalavyatirekī — has only negative (vyatireka) vyāpti. E.g., inferences about the self or absolute entities where positive instances cannot be enumerated.

3. Anvaya-vyatirekī — has both positive and negative vyāpti. E.g., "The mountain has fire, because of smoke" — both "where there is smoke there is fire" and "where there is no fire there is no smoke" hold.

Q23

सव्यभिचारं निरूपयत।

Explain Savyabhicāra (the fallacy of the irregular/erratic hetu).

Answer — Sanskrit

अनैकान्तिकः सव्यभिचारः। यः हेतुः साध्यवति, असाध्यवति च विद्यते — सः सव्यभिचारः।

सव्यभिचारः त्रिविधः — साधारणः, असाधारणः, अनुपसंहारी च।

साधारणः — "पर्वतो वह्निमान् प्रमेयत्वात्।" प्रमेयत्वं वह्निमति (महानसे) वह्न्यभाववति (ह्रदे) च वर्तते — साध्यवति असाध्यवति च।

असाधारणः — "शब्दः नित्यः शब्दत्वात्।" शब्दत्वं केवलं शब्दे एव वर्तते, न नित्येषु (आकाशादिषु) न अनित्येषु (घटादिषु) — सपक्षविपक्षव्यावृत्तम्।

अनुपसंहारी — "सर्वं अनित्यं प्रमेयत्वात्।" अत्र सर्वम् एव पक्षः, अतः दृष्टान्तः (सपक्षः विपक्षः वा) न सम्भवति।

Answer — English

Savyabhicāra is the "erratic" or "irregular" hetu — one that is present in both sapakṣa (where sādhya obtains) and vipakṣa (where sādhya does not obtain), and hence lacks vyāpti.

Three sub-kinds: (1) Sādhāraṇa (too wide) — present in both sapakṣa and vipakṣa. E.g., "The mountain has fire because of knowability (prameya-tva)" — knowability is present in fire-possessing places (kitchen) and also in non-fire places (lake), so it proves nothing. (2) Asādhāraṇa (too narrow) — present only in the pakṣa itself, absent from both sapakṣa and vipakṣa. E.g., "Sound is eternal because of soundness (śabdatva)" — soundness belongs only to sound, so no sapakṣa or vipakṣa example exists. (3) Anupasaṃhāri — the entire universe is taken as the pakṣa, so no example (sapakṣa or vipakṣa) can be given at all. E.g., "Everything is non-eternal because of knowability."

Q24

उपमानं निरूपयत।

Explain Upamāna (comparison as a pramāṇa).

Answer — Sanskrit

उपमानं नाम उपमितिकरणम्। सादृश्यज्ञानात् संज्ञासंज्ञिसम्बन्धज्ञानम् उपमितिः। तत्करणम् उपमानम्।

प्रक्रिया — पुरुषः वनं गच्छति। तत्र "गवयः गोसदृशः" इति पूर्वं श्रुतम् अस्ति। वने गवयं दृष्ट्वा "अयं गोसदृशः अतः गवयः" इति संज्ञासंज्ञिसम्बन्धः ज्ञायते। एतत् उपमितिः।

उपमाने व्यापारः सादृश्यस्मृतिः। उपमानं न अनुमानान्तर्गतम् — यतः अत्र व्याप्तिज्ञानम् नास्ति।

Answer — English

Upamāna is the pramāṇa (means of valid knowledge) that produces upamiti — the cognition of the relation between a name and its object through perceived similarity.

Process: A person goes to a forest. He has previously heard "a gavaya (wild cow) looks like a cow." On seeing the gavaya in the forest, he cognises "this is similar to a cow, so this must be what is called a gavaya." This cognition of the name-object relation is upamiti.

The operative function (vyāpāra) in upamāna is the recollection of similarity (sādṛśya-smṛti). Upamāna is a distinct pramāṇa — it cannot be reduced to anumāna, because it does not rely on vyāpti.

Q25

शक्तं पदम् — व्याख्यात।

Explain "Śaktaṃ padam" — a word endowed with denotative power.

Answer — Sanskrit

ईश्वरसंकेतः शक्तिः। येन पदेन यः अर्थः ईश्वरेण संकेतितः, तस्य पदस्य सः अर्थः शक्यार्थः। तद् पदं शक्तं पदम् इत्युच्यते।

पदार्थबोधस्य साधनत्रयम् — शक्तिः, लक्षणा, व्यञ्जना च। तेषु शक्तिः मुख्या। शक्त्या एव मुख्यार्थः बोध्यते।

यत्र शक्यार्थः बाधितः तत्र लक्षणाशक्तिः आश्रीयते। यथा — "गङ्गायां घोषः" इत्यत्र "गङ्गा" पदस्य शक्यार्थः नद्याः प्रवाहः। परं तत्र घोषः सम्भवति — अतः लक्षणया तीरम् अर्थः।

Answer — English

Śakti is the divinely ordained (Īśvara-saṃketa) denotative power — the God-given relation between a word and its primary meaning. A word possessing this power is called śaktaṃ padam.

There are three functions by which a word conveys meaning: (1) Śakti (primary denotation), (2) Lakṣaṇā (secondary/indicative), (3) Vyañjanā (suggestion). Among them, śakti is primary.

Example: The word "gaṅgā" by its śakti denotes the river (stream). But "gaṅgāyāṃ ghoṣaḥ" (the village is on the Gaṅgā) — here the primary meaning (stream) is blocked, so lakṣaṇā shifts the meaning to the bank (tīra).

Q26

वाक्यं द्विविधम् — व्याख्यात।

Vākya is of two kinds — Explain.

Answer — Sanskrit

वाक्यं द्विविधम् — विधिवाक्यम्, निषेधवाक्यञ्च।

विधिवाक्यम् — यत् वाक्यम् अनुष्ठानाय प्रेरयति। यथा — "सोमेन यजेत" (सोमेन यजनं विधीयते)।

निषेधवाक्यम् — यत् वाक्यम् अनुष्ठानात् निवारयति। यथा — "कलञ्जं न भक्षयेत्" (कलञ्जभक्षणं निषिध्यते)।

वाक्यस्य त्रयो गुणाः — आकाङ्क्षा (syntactic expectancy), योग्यता (semantic compatibility), आसत्तिः (proximity). एतैः त्रिभिः गुणैः युक्तः पदसमूहः एव वाक्यम्।

Answer — English

Vākya (sentence) is of two kinds: Vidhi-vākya (injunctive) and Niṣedha-vākya (prohibitive).

Vidhi-vākya prescribes an action. E.g., "Sacrifice with soma" — enjoining the performance of the soma sacrifice.

Niṣedha-vākya prohibits an action. E.g., "Do not eat kalañja (garlic/onion)" — a Vedic prohibition.

A valid vākya requires three properties in its constituent words: ākāṅkṣā (each word expecting the others for completeness), yogyatā (the meanings must be mutually compatible), and āsatti/sannidhi (the words must be uttered in temporal proximity).

Q27

लक्षणा।

Write a short note on Lakṣaṇā (secondary signification).

Answer — Sanskrit

मुख्यार्थबाधे तत्सम्बन्धे रूढितः प्रयोजनाद्वा यः अर्थः बोध्यते सा लक्षणा।

लक्षणायाः त्रयः भेदाः — जहल्लक्षणा, अजहल्लक्षणा, जहदजहल्लक्षणा च।

जहल्लक्षणा — मुख्यार्थं जहाति। "गङ्गायां घोषः" — गङ्गायाः मुख्यार्थः (प्रवाहः) जहाति, तीरम् अर्थः।

अजहल्लक्षणा — मुख्यार्थं न जहाति। "शौरिः विक्रान्तः" — श्रीकृष्णस्य शौर्यं मुख्यार्थेन सह लक्ष्यते।

जहदजहल्लक्षणा — "तत्त्वमसि" — "तत्" पदस्य सर्वज्ञत्वम्, "त्वम्" पदस्य अल्पज्ञत्वम् जहाति, शुद्धचैतन्यमात्रम् अर्थः।

Answer — English

Lakṣaṇā is the secondary (indicative) function of a word — operative when the primary (śakya) meaning is blocked, and conveying a related meaning either by convention or by purpose.

Three kinds: (1) Jahallakṣaṇā — completely abandons the primary meaning. E.g., "gaṅgāyāṃ ghoṣaḥ" where "gaṅgā" shifts from "stream" to "bank." (2) Ajahallakṣaṇā — retains the primary meaning, adds a secondary one. (3) Jahadajahallakṣaṇā — partially retains and partially abandons, as in the great Upaniṣadic statement "Tat tvam asi" (That thou art), where the conditioned aspects of both terms are negated and pure consciousness alone is indicated.

Q28

शाब्दबोधे सहकारिणः।

What are the auxiliary factors (sahakāriṇas) in Śābdabodha (verbal/linguistic cognition)?

Answer — Sanskrit

शाब्दबोधस्य सहकारिकारणानि — आकाङ्क्षा, योग्यता, आसत्तिः, तात्पर्यञ्च।

आकाङ्क्षा — एकस्य पदस्य अन्यपदापेक्षा। यथा — "पच" इति पदं कर्तृ-कर्म-पदापेक्षं करोति।

योग्यता — पदार्थानाम् परस्परबाधाभावः। "अग्निना सिञ्चति" इति वाक्ये योग्यता नास्ति।

आसत्तिः — पदानां कालिकसान्निध्यम्। विच्छिन्नोच्चारणे वाक्यं न भवति।

तात्पर्यम् — वक्तुः अभिप्रायः। शाब्दबोधाय वक्तुः तात्पर्यज्ञानम् आवश्यकम्।

Answer — English

The auxiliary factors (sahakāri-kāraṇas) in śābdabodha (cognition generated by verbal testimony) are four: ākāṅkṣā, yogyatā, āsatti (sannidhi), and tātparya.

Ākāṅkṣā — syntactic expectancy: each word expects others to complete the sense. "Cook!" expects a subject and object.

Yogyatā — semantic compatibility: the meanings must not contradict each other. "Water with fire" lacks yogyatā.

Āsatti — temporal proximity: words must be uttered in close succession; a sentence split across days fails.

Tātparya — the speaker's intention: knowing what the speaker means is essential to correctly understand the sentence.

Q29

अनुमानप्रमाणं निरूपयत।

Explain the pramāṇa of Anumāna (inference) in detail.

Answer — Sanskrit

अनुमानस्वरूपम्
अनुमानं नाम व्याप्तिविशिष्टपक्षधर्मताज्ञानजन्यं ज्ञानम्। व्याप्तिज्ञानात् पक्षधर्मताज्ञानात् च परामर्शः उत्पद्यते। परामर्शात् अनुमितिः।

अनुमानस्य प्रक्रिया
१. साध्यः — यः अर्थः अनुमातुं वाञ्छितः (यथा वह्निः)।
२. पक्षः — यत्र साध्यः सिषाधयिषितः (यथा पर्वतः)।
३. हेतुः (लिङ्गम्) — साध्येन व्याप्तः पक्षे वर्तमानः धर्मः (यथा धूमः)।
४. व्याप्तिः — हेतोः साध्येन अव्यभिचरितसम्बन्धः।
५. परामर्शः — "पर्वते धूमः वह्निव्याप्तः" इति ज्ञानम्।
६. अनुमितिः — "पर्वतो वह्निमान्" इति ज्ञानम्।

अनुमानभेदाः
अनुमानं द्विविधम् — स्वार्थानुमानम्, परार्थानुमानञ्च।
स्वार्थानुमाने — स्वस्य ज्ञानार्थम् अनुमानम्।
परार्थानुमाने — परस्मै बोधनाय पञ्चावयवी प्रयुज्यते — प्रतिज्ञा, हेतुः, उदाहरणम्, उपनयः, निगमनञ्च।

हेतोः लक्षणम्
हेतोः पञ्च लक्षणानि — पक्षधर्मता, सपक्षसत्त्वम्, विपक्षाव्यावृत्तिः, अबाधितविषयता, असत्प्रतिपक्षता च।

हेत्वाभासाः
अनुमानस्य दोषाः पञ्च — सव्यभिचारः, विरुद्धः, सत्प्रतिपक्षः, असिद्धः, बाधितः च।

उपसंहारः
अनुमानम् प्रत्यक्षानुपलब्धार्थस्य ज्ञानसाधनम्। तर्कशास्त्रे अनुमानं सर्वप्रधानम्।

Answer — English

Nature of Anumāna
Anumāna (inference) is the cognition generated by parāmarśa — the joint cognition of the hetu as residing in the pakṣa and as pervaded by vyāpti with the sādhya. It is the second of the four pramāṇas in Nyāya.

Process of Inference
(1) Sādhya — the probandum, what is to be proved (e.g., fire). (2) Pakṣa — the subject where sādhya is to be established (e.g., mountain). (3) Hetu/Liṅga — the probans, present in pakṣa and pervaded by sādhya (e.g., smoke). (4) Vyāpti — the invariable concomitance between hetu and sādhya ("wherever smoke, there fire"). (5) Parāmarśa — the cognition "the smoke on the mountain is pervaded by fire." (6) Anumiti — the resulting cognition "the mountain has fire."

Kinds of Anumāna
Svārthānumāna — inference for oneself (the mental process, not formally stated). Parārthānumāna — inference stated for another, using the five-membered syllogism (pañcāvayavī): (1) Pratijñā (proposition), (2) Hetu (reason), (3) Udāharaṇa (example), (4) Upanaya (application), (5) Nigamana (conclusion). E.g.: "The mountain has fire (1); because of smoke (2); whatever has smoke has fire, like a kitchen (3); the mountain has smoke (4); therefore the mountain has fire (5)."

Five Properties of a Valid Hetu
Pakṣa-dharmatā (presence in pakṣa), sapakṣa-sattva (presence in similar cases), vipakṣa-vyāvṛtti (absence in dissimilar cases), abādhita-viṣayatā (not contradicted by other pramāṇas), and asat-pratipakṣatā (not counter-balanced).

Fallacies (Hetvābhāsas)
Five: Savyabhicāra (irregular), Viruddha (contradictory), Satpratipakṣa (counter-balanced), Asiddha (unproved), Bādhita (contradicted).

Conclusion
Anumāna is the instrument for knowing what cannot be directly perceived. It is the cornerstone of Nyāya epistemology and logical reasoning.

Q30

हेत्वाभासाः।

Discuss the Hetvābhāsas (fallacies of the probans) in detail.

Answer — Sanskrit

हेत्वाभासस्वरूपम्
हेतुवत् आभासते, परन्तु वास्तवेन हेतुः न भवति — इति हेत्वाभासः। हेत्वाभासाः पञ्च सन्ति।

१. सव्यभिचारः (अनैकान्तिकः)
साध्यवति असाध्यवति च विद्यमानः हेतुः सव्यभिचारः।
— साधारणः: "पर्वतो वह्निमान् प्रमेयत्वात्।" प्रमेयत्वं वह्निमति (महानसे) वह्न्यभाववति (ह्रदे) च।
— असाधारणः: "शब्दः नित्यः शब्दत्वात्।" शब्दत्वं शब्दमात्रे — सपक्षविपक्षव्यावृत्तम्।
— अनुपसंहारी: "सर्वं अनित्यं प्रमेयत्वात्।" सर्वस्यापि पक्षत्वात् दृष्टान्तो नास्ति।

२. विरुद्धः
साध्याभावं साधयति। यथा — "शब्दः नित्यः कृतकत्वात्।" कृतकत्वं तु अनित्यत्वव्याप्तम्।

३. सत्प्रतिपक्षः
यस्य हेतोः प्रतिपक्षेऽपि सत् हेत्वन्तरम् अस्ति। यथा — "शब्दः नित्यः श्रावणत्वात्। शब्दः अनित्यः कृतकत्वात्।" — एतौ द्वौ विरुद्धौ हेतू।

४. असिद्धः
पक्षे हेतोः सिद्धत्वाभावः। त्रिविधः — आश्रयासिद्धः, स्वरूपासिद्धः, व्याप्यत्वासिद्धः।
— आश्रयासिद्धः: "गगनपुष्पं सुरभि, द्रव्यत्वात्।" गगनपुष्पमेव नास्ति।
— स्वरूपासिद्धः: "शब्दः नित्यः स्पर्शवत्त्वात्।" शब्दे स्पर्शः नास्ति।
— व्याप्यत्वासिद्धः: "वह्निः धूमवान् धूमे सति।" धूमः वह्निव्याप्तः नास्ति।

५. बाधितः
यः हेतुः अन्यप्रमाणबाधितसाध्यसाधकः। यथा — "वह्निः शीतः द्रव्यत्वात्।" वह्नेः शैत्यम् अनुमानेन गम्यते, परन्तु प्रत्यक्षबाधितम्।

उपसंहारः
एते पञ्च हेत्वाभासाः अनुमानदोषाः। तान् परिहृत्य शुद्धहेतुना अनुमानं कर्तव्यम्।

Answer — English

Nature of Hetvābhāsa
A hetvābhāsa appears to be a valid hetu (probans) but is actually fallacious — it violates one or more conditions of a valid hetu. The Nyāya system enumerates five hetvābhāsas.

1. Savyabhicāra (Irregular/Erratic)
The hetu occurs in both sapakṣa (where sādhya holds) and vipakṣa (where sādhya does not hold) — lacking vyāpti. Three sub-kinds: (1) Sādhāraṇa (too wide) — e.g., "The mountain has fire because of knowability" — knowability is present in fire-possessing places and also in non-fire places like a lake, so it wanders across both. (2) Asādhāraṇa (too narrow) — e.g., "Sound is eternal because of soundness (śabdatva)" — soundness belongs only to sound, absent from both sapakṣa and vipakṣa, so it generates only doubt. (3) Anupasaṃhāri — e.g., "Everything is non-eternal because of knowability" — since everything is the pakṣa, no sapakṣa or vipakṣa example can be provided.

2. Viruddha (Contradictory)
The hetu is pervaded by the contradictory of the sādhya — it proves the opposite of what is intended. E.g., "Sound is eternal because it is produced" — "producedness" actually proves non-eternality.

3. Satpratipakṣa (Counter-balanced)
A genuine counter-hetu of equal strength exists, leading to opposing conclusions simultaneously. Neither inference can be accepted. E.g., "Sound is eternal because of audibility" versus "Sound is non-eternal because of producedness."

4. Asiddha (Unestablished)
The hetu is not established in the pakṣa. Three sub-kinds: Āśrayāsiddha (the pakṣa itself does not exist — e.g., "sky-flower"); Svarūpāsiddha (the hetu does not exist in the pakṣa — e.g., "sound has touch"); Vyāpyatvāsiddha (the hetu's pervadedness is not established).

5. Bādhita (Contradicted)
The hetu's sādhya is already contradicted by a stronger pramāṇa (usually perception). E.g., "Fire is cold because it is a substance" — the sādhya (coldness of fire) is directly refuted by perception.

Conclusion
The five hetvābhāsas cover all possible ways a hetu can fail. A logician must identify and avoid these to produce a sound (saddhetu) inference.

Q31

उपमानम्।

Discuss Upamāna (comparison/analogy) as a pramāṇa in detail.

Answer — Sanskrit

उपमानस्वरूपम्
उपमानं सादृश्यज्ञानजन्यसंज्ञासंज्ञिसम्बन्धज्ञानकरणम्। तज्जन्यं ज्ञानं उपमितिः।

उपमितेः प्रक्रिया
(१) पूर्वमेव "गवयः गोसदृशः" इति श्रुतम्।
(२) वने गवयं दृष्ट्वा सादृश्यं स्मृतम् — "अयं गोसदृशः।"
(३) तदनन्तरं "अयमेव गवयशब्दवाच्यः" इति संज्ञासंज्ञिसम्बन्धः ज्ञायते।
एतत् उपमितिज्ञानम्।

उपमाने व्यापारः
सादृश्यस्मृतिः उपमाने व्यापारः। आप्तवाक्यजन्यसादृश्यज्ञानस्मृत्या संज्ञासंज्ञिसम्बन्धज्ञानं जायते।

उपमानस्य स्वातन्त्र्यम्
नैयायिकाः उपमानं स्वतन्त्रं प्रमाणम् इति स्वीकुर्वन्ति। बौद्धाः तु उपमानम् अनुमानान्तर्गतम् इति वदन्ति। परन्तु नैयायिकमते — अत्र व्याप्तिज्ञानं नास्ति, सादृश्यज्ञानं कारणम् — अतः उपमानं स्वतन्त्रम्।

उपमितिः
का उपमितिः? संज्ञासंज्ञिसम्बन्धविषयकं ज्ञानम् उपमितिः। "अयं गवयः" इति ज्ञानम् उपमितिः।

उपमानस्य उपयोगः
अज्ञातार्थज्ञाने उपमानं प्रमाणम्। पशुपक्षिनामज्ञाने, अपरिचितव्यक्तिपरिचये च उपमानम् उपयुज्यते।

उपसंहारः
उपमानं प्रत्यक्ष-अनुमान-शब्दव्यतिरिक्तम् स्वतन्त्रं प्रमाणम्। नैयायिकचतुष्टयप्रमाणेषु तृतीयस्थाने विराजते।

Answer — English

Nature of Upamāna
Upamāna is the pramāṇa whose result (upamiti) is the cognition of the relation between a name (saṃjñā) and its referent (saṃjñī), produced through the cognition of similarity (sādṛśya).

Process of Upamiti
(1) One has previously heard from a trustworthy person: "A gavaya looks like a cow." (2) On going to the forest, one perceives an animal and cognises the similarity: "This looks like a cow." (3) Through this similarity-cognition, one recalls the description and arrives at: "This is what is called a gavaya." This final cognition — of the word–object relation — is upamiti.

Operative Function (Vyāpāra)
The vyāpāra in upamāna is sādṛśya-smṛti — the memory of the similarity as communicated by verbal testimony. It is this smṛti, combined with direct perception of the similar object, that generates the upamiti.

Upamāna as an Independent Pramāṇa
The Naiyāyikas accept upamāna as a fourth, independent pramāṇa. Buddhists reduce it to anumāna. The Naiyāyika counter: no vyāpti is operative here — the mediating cognition is similarity, not invariable concomitance — therefore upamāna is irreducibly distinct.

Definition of Upamiti
Upamiti is the cognition whose content is the name-object relation (saṃjñā-saṃjñi-sambandha). "This is a gavaya" — the first-time recognition via comparison — is upamiti.

Practical Use
Upamāna is invaluable for learning the names of unfamiliar animals, plants, and people through resemblance to known entities.

Conclusion
Upamāna is the third of the four Nyāya pramāṇas — distinct from perception, inference, and verbal testimony. Its unique character lies in the role of perceived similarity as the means to new propositional knowledge.

Q32

शाब्दबोधहेतुः।

Discuss the causes (hetus) of Śābdabodha (verbal cognition).

Answer — Sanskrit

शाब्दबोधस्वरूपम्
शब्दप्रमाणजन्यं ज्ञानं शाब्दबोधः। आप्तोक्तेः शब्दात् यत् ज्ञानम् उत्पद्यते तत् शाब्दबोधः।

शाब्दबोधस्य हेतवः
शाब्दबोधस्य प्रधानहेतुः — वाक्यस्य पदानां शक्तिः। तस्याः सहकारिकारणानि — आकाङ्क्षा, योग्यता, आसत्तिः, तात्पर्यञ्च।

१. शक्तिः
ईश्वरसंकेतः शक्तिः। शक्त्या एव पदं स्वार्थं बोधयति। "घट" पदं शक्त्या घटम् एव बोधयति।

२. आकाङ्क्षा
एकस्य पदस्य अन्यपदापेक्षा। "आनय" इति पदं "किम्" इति अपेक्षते। आकाङ्क्षारहिते वाक्यं न भवति।

३. योग्यता
पदार्थानां परस्परबाधाभावः। "अग्निना सिञ्चति" इत्यत्र अग्निः सेचने अयोग्यः — योग्यता नास्ति, अतः शाब्दबोधो न जायते।

४. आसत्तिः (सान्निध्यम्)
पदानां कालिकसान्निध्यम्। यदि "गां" इत्युक्त्वा वर्षानन्तरं "आनय" उच्यते तदा वाक्यं न भवति।

५. तात्पर्यम्
वक्तुः अभिप्रायः। एकस्यैव वाक्यस्य अनेकार्थे सम्भवे वक्तुः तात्पर्यज्ञानम् अर्थनिर्णये साहाय्यं करोति।

उपसंहारः
शक्तिर्मुख्या। आकाङ्क्षा-योग्यता-आसत्ति-तात्पर्याणि सहकारिणः। एतेषां समवायेन शाब्दबोधः जायते।

Answer — English

Nature of Śābdabodha
Śābdabodha is the cognition generated by verbal testimony (śabda-pramāṇa) — the knowledge one gains from a reliable (āpta) speaker's utterance.

Primary Cause: Śakti
The primary cause of śābdabodha is the śakti (denotative power) of each word — its God-ordained relation to its meaning. "Pot" (ghaṭa) denotes a pot by śakti.

Auxiliary Causes (Sahakāriṇas)

1. Ākāṅkṣā (Syntactic Expectancy) — Each word in a sentence expects other words to complete the intended sense. "Bring!" expects "what?" Without ākāṅkṣā, words are mere isolated utterances, not a sentence.

2. Yogyatā (Semantic Compatibility) — The meanings of words must not contradict each other. "Water the plants with fire" — fire and watering are semantically incompatible; no valid śābdabodha arises.

3. Āsatti / Sannidhi (Temporal Proximity) — Words must be uttered in close temporal sequence. If "bring" is uttered one year after "cow," no cognitive unit (vākya) is formed.

4. Tātparya (Speaker's Intention) — Where a sentence is ambiguous, the speaker's intended meaning resolves the ambiguity. E.g., "saindhava" can mean both "horse" and "salt" — context and speaker's intent determine which is meant.

Conclusion
Śakti is the primary cause; ākāṅkṣā, yogyatā, āsatti, and tātparya are indispensable auxiliaries. Their joint operation produces the valid verbal cognition that is śābdabodha.

Q33

शाब्दबोधः।

Write a detailed essay on Śābdabodha (verbal cognition / cognition from word-testimony).

Answer — Sanskrit

शाब्दबोधस्वरूपम्
शब्दजन्यं ज्ञानं शाब्दबोधः। शब्दः नाम आप्तोपदिष्टः व्याकरणसम्मतः पदसमूहः। तस्माद् उत्पन्नं बोधनं शाब्दबोधः।

शब्दप्रमाणस्य स्वरूपम्
शब्दः द्विविधः — वैदिकः, लौकिकः च। वैदिकशब्दः ईश्वरप्रणीतः — अपौरुषेयः। लौकिकशब्दः आप्तमनुष्यप्रणीतः। उभाभ्यां शाब्दबोधः सम्भवति।

पदस्य अर्थबोधनप्रक्रिया
प्रत्येकं पदं शक्त्या स्वार्थं बोधयति। पदानां संयोगे वाक्यं जायते। वाक्यात् वाक्यार्थबोधः। वाक्यार्थः — "कारकसम्बन्धविशिष्टक्रियाविषयकं बोधनम्।"

शाब्दबोधस्य साधनानि
शाब्दबोधाय पदस्य शक्तिः मुख्या। सहकारिणः — आकाङ्क्षा, योग्यता, आसत्तिः, तात्पर्यञ्च।
— आकाङ्क्षा: पदानाम् अन्योन्यापेक्षा।
— योग्यता: पदार्थानां सङ्गतता।
— आसत्तिः: उच्चारणे कालिकसान्निध्यम्।
— तात्पर्यम्: वक्तुः विवक्षा।

शाब्दबोधे लक्षणाया भूमिका
यत्र शक्यार्थः बाधितः तत्र लक्षणाशक्त्या अर्थः बोध्यते। "गङ्गायां घोषः" — गङ्गाशब्दस्य शक्यार्थः प्रवाहः, परन्तु लक्षणया तीरम् अर्थः गृह्यते।

शाब्दबोधस्य प्रकाराः
— मुख्यार्थबोधः (शक्त्या)।
— लाक्षणिकार्थबोधः (लक्षणया)।
— व्यङ्ग्यार्थबोधः (व्यञ्जनया)।

शाब्दबोधस्य महत्त्वम्
वेदागमपुराणादिग्रन्थेभ्यः ज्ञानप्राप्तौ शब्दप्रमाणं परमावश्यकम्। गुरूपदेशेन शिष्यः ज्ञानं प्राप्नोति — एतत् सर्वं शाब्दबोधपरम्परायाः फलम्।

उपसंहारः
शाब्दबोधः न केवलं ज्ञानसाधनम् — अपितु भारतीयज्ञानपरम्पराया मूलाधारः। तर्कशास्त्रे, मीमांसायां, वेदान्ते च शाब्दबोधस्य विचारः अत्यन्तं महत्त्वपूर्णः।

Answer — English

Nature of Śābdabodha
Śābdabodha is the valid cognition produced by verbal testimony (śabda). It arises when a grammatically well-formed sentence, uttered by a reliable speaker (āpta), is heard and understood.

Two Kinds of Śabda
Vaidika śabda — the Vedas, which the Naiyāyikas consider to be authored by an omniscient God (Īśvara), hence impersonal and infallible. Laukika śabda — statements of reliable human speakers in everyday life. Both generate valid śābdabodha.

How Words Produce Meaning
Each word conveys its own meaning through its śakti (denotative power). A collection of words with ākāṅkṣā, yogyatā, and āsatti forms a vākya (sentence). The vākya generates vākyārtha-bodha — cognition of the complete propositional content, understood as: cognition of an action qualified by its kāraka (case) relations.

Causes of Śābdabodha
Primary: śakti of each word. Auxiliaries: (1) Ākāṅkṣā — mutual expectancy of words; (2) Yogyatā — semantic non-contradiction; (3) Āsatti — temporal proximity of utterance; (4) Tātparya — speaker's intention, resolving ambiguity.

Role of Lakṣaṇā in Śābdabodha
When the śakya (primary denotative) meaning is blocked, lakṣaṇā (secondary/indicative function) operates. "Gaṅgāyāṃ ghoṣaḥ" — "on the Gaṅgā" — shifts from the primary meaning (stream) to the related meaning (bank), because a village cannot exist in a river-stream.

Three Modes of Verbal Meaning
(1) Mukhyārtha-bodha via śakti (primary denotation), (2) Lākṣaṇikārtha-bodha via lakṣaṇā (secondary), (3) Vyaṅgyārtha-bodha via vyañjanā (suggestion/implication).

Significance
Śābdabodha is the foundation of all scriptural and traditional knowledge transmission — from the Vedas to the guru-śiṣya paramparā. Without the validity of verbal testimony, no received knowledge would be epistemologically grounded.

Conclusion
Śābdabodha is not merely one pramāṇa among four — it is the backbone of the Indian intellectual tradition. Its analysis in Nyāya, Mīmāṃsā, and Vedānta represents one of the most sophisticated philosophies of language in the world.
