Nyāya & Pramāṇas

न्यायः

Nyāya — The System of Logic
Definitionप्रमाणैः अर्थपरीक्षणं न्यायः।

Nyāya is the examination of objects by means of valid instruments of knowledge (pramāṇas). The Nyāya system recognises four pramāṇas:

#पमाणम्NameMeaning
1प्रत्यक्षम्PratyakṣaDirect perception
2अनुमानम्AnumānaInference
3उपमानम्UpamānaComparison / analogy
4शब्दःŚabdaVerbal testimony

Anumāna follows pratyakṣa and precedes upamāna. It is the most analysed pramāṇa in the Tarka Sopanam syllabus.

INFERENCE

Anumāna & Anumiti

अनुमानम् · अनुमितिः

Anumāna (Inference) & Anumiti (Inferential Cognition)
Mūlam (textbook) अनुमितिकरणमनुमानम् ।
परामर्शजन्यज्ञानमनुमितिः ।

अनुमानम् — the instrument (karaṇa) that produces anumiti. It is the pramāṇa that gives knowledge of what cannot be directly perceived, by means of a mark (liṅga/hetu).

अनुमितिः — the result (phala): the fresh inferential cognition of the sādhya in the pakṣa. It is born of parāmarśa.

The full sequence:

StepSanskritWhat happens
1धूमदर्शनम्Smoke seen on the mountain
2व्याप्तिस्मरणम्Recall: "wherever smoke, there fire"
3परामर्शः"वह्निव्याप्यधूमवान् अयं पर्वतः" — this mountain has fire-pervaded smoke
4अनुमितिः"पर्वतो वह्निमान्" — the mountain has fire ✓

Key roles: Anumāna = karaṇa (instrument). Parāmarśa = vyāpāra (operative act). Anumiti = phala (result/cognition).

KEY TERMS

Pakṣa, Sādhya, Hetu, Dṛṣṭānta

पारिभाषिकाः शब्दाः

Technical Terms of Inference
TermSanskrit definitionEnglishExample
पक्षः सन्दिग्धसाध्यवान् धर्मी Subject — where sādhya is to be proved Mountain (पर्वतः)
साध्यम् यत् साध्यते तत् साध्यम् What is to be proved (major term) Fire (वह्निः)
हेतुः पक्षे साध्यस्य साधने किञ्चित् निमित्तम् Probans / reason (minor term) Smoke (धूमः)
दृष्टान्तः साध्यसाधनसाहचर्यदृष्टस्थलम् Example where both sādhya and hetu are seen Kitchen (महानसम्)
सपक्षः साध्यवान् पक्षभिन्नः Similar instance (where sādhya exists) Kitchen (has fire)
विपक्षः साध्याभाववान् Dissimilar instance (where sādhya absent) Lake (no fire)

The inference "पर्वतो वह्निमान् धूमात्" maps as: pakṣa = mountain, sādhya = fire, hetu = smoke, dṛṣṭānta = kitchen.

INFERENCE

Parāmarśa

परामर्शः

Parāmarśa — The Operative Cognition
Mūlam (textbook) व्याप्तिविशिष्टपक्षधर्मताज्ञानं परामर्शः ।
यथा — वह्निव्याप्यधूमवान् अयं पर्वतः इति ज्ञानं परामर्शः ।
तज्जन्यं पर्वतो वह्निमान् इति ज्ञानमनुमितिः ।

Parāmarśa is the cognition in which:

ComponentWhat is cognised
पक्षधर्मताThe hetu (smoke) is present in the pakṣa (mountain)
व्याप्तिःThat very hetu is pervaded by the sādhya (fire)

Both are grasped together in one cognition — that is parāmarśa. It is the vyāpāra (operative act) of anumāna and the immediate (antima) cause of anumiti.

The textbook defines parāmarśa's two viṣayas (objects) as pakṣadharmatā and vyāpti — which is also a 1-mark answer (Q13).

INFERENCE

Vyāpti, Pāka, Saugamyatā

व्याप्तिः

Vyāpti — Invariable Concomitance
Definitionअव्यभिचरितसहचरणं व्याप्तिः — हेतोः साध्येन अनिवार्यसम्बन्धः।
KindSanskritFormExample
Positiveअन्वयव्याप्तिःWherever hetu → there sādhyaWherever smoke → fire
Negativeव्यतिरेकव्याप्तिःWherever no sādhya → no hetuWherever no fire → no smoke

Vyāpti is the foundation of anumāna. Without it, the hetu has no inferential force.

पाकः · सौगम्यता

Pāka & Saugamyatā — Methods of Grasping Vyāpti
Pākaबहुशः दर्शनजनितसंस्कारेण व्याप्तिनिश्चयः पाकः।

पाकः — the firm cognition of vyāpti arising from the mental impression (saṃskāra) left by repeated co-observation of hetu and sādhya. Like knowing "smoke always goes with fire" after seeing it many times.

सौगम्यता — one specific type (bheda) of pāka; the easy or smooth method of grasping vyāpti.

INFERENCE

Liṅga — Three Kinds of Hetu

लिङ्गं त्रिविधम्

The Three Kinds of Inferential Mark
KindSanskritDefinitionExample
1 केवलान्वयी Has only anvaya-vyāpti (no known vipakṣa) इदं वक्तव्यम् अभिधेयत्वात्
2 केवलव्यतिरेकी Has only vyatireka-vyāpti (no usable sapakṣa) Inferences about absolute entities
3 अन्वयव्यतिरेकी Has both anvaya and vyatireka vyāpti पर्वतो वह्निमान् धूमात्

Most common inferences (like the mountain-fire example) use the anvaya-vyatirekī hetu.

FALLACIES

Hetvābhāsas

हेत्वाभासाः

The Five Fallacies of the Probans
Mūlam (textbook) सव्यभिचार-विरुद्ध-सत्प्रतिपक्ष-असिद्ध-वाधिताः पञ्च हेत्वाभासाः ।

A hetvābhāsa appears to be a valid hetu but is not — हेतुवत् आभासते परन्तु न हेतुः. The word itself means "hetu-dosa" (fault of the hetu).

#NameFaultTextbook Example
1 सव्यभिचारः
(अनैकान्तिकः)		 Hetu present in both sapakṣa and vipakṣa — lacks vyāpti पर्वतो वह्निमान् प्रमेयत्वात्
(prameya = knowable, present everywhere)
2 विरुद्धः Hetu proves the opposite of what is intended शब्दः नित्यः कृतकत्वात्
(producedness proves non-eternality)
3 सत्प्रतिपक्षः A counter-hetu of equal strength exists — draws opposite conclusion शब्दः नित्यः श्रावणत्वात् vs. शब्दः अनित्यः कृतकत्वात्
4 असिद्धः Hetu not established in the pakṣa (3 sub-kinds) गगनपुष्पं सुरभि द्रव्यत्वात्
(pakṣa itself doesn't exist)
5 बाधितः Sādhya already contradicted by a stronger pramāṇa वह्निः शीतः द्रव्यत्वात्
(refuted by direct perception)

Sub-kindDefectTextbook Example

अनुपसंहारी Entire universe is pakṣa — no example possible at all सर्वं अनित्यं प्रमेयत्वात्

Sub-kindDefectExample

PRAMĀṆA

Upamāna

उपमानम्

Upamāna — Comparison as a Pramāṇa
Definitionसादृश्यज्ञानात् संज्ञासंज्ञिसम्बन्धज्ञानं उपमितिः । तत्करणं उपमानम् ।
TermMeaning
उपमानम्The pramāṇa (instrument) — comparison
उपमितिःThe result — cognition of a name-object relation
व्यापारःसादृश्यस्मृतिः — recollection of similarity

Process: (1) Hear from a reliable person: "a gavaya looks like a cow." (2) Go to the forest, see an animal resembling a cow. (3) Recall the similarity. (4) Cognise: "this is what is called a gavaya" — that is upamiti.

Upamāna is independent from anumāna because no vyāpti is involved — only perceived similarity mediates the cognition.

CAUSATION

Kāraṇa

कारणम् — त्रिविधम्

Kāraṇa — The Three Kinds of Cause
Definitionकार्यनियतपूर्ववृत्ति कारणम् ।

A cause is that which invariably precedes its effect (kārya). It has three kinds:

KindSanskritMeaningExample
1 समवायिकारणम् Inherent / material cause — in which the effect inheres Threads (tantu) for a cloth (paṭa)
2 असमवायिकारणम् Non-inherent cause — present in the material cause The conjunction of threads
3 निमित्तकारणम् Efficient / instrumental cause — external agent The weaver's skill and loom

The textbook also adds that a cause must be ananyathāsiddha — not explicable otherwise (i.e., it is genuinely necessary for the effect).

EPISTEMOLOGY

Anubhūti

अनुभूतिः

Anubhūti — Direct Apprehension
Definitionस्मृतिव्यतिरिक्तं ज्ञानम् अनुभूतिः ।

Anubhūti is any cognition that is not memory (smṛti). It includes perception (pratyakṣa), inference (anumāna), comparison (upamāna), and testimony (śabda) — all fresh, first-hand cognitions.

TypeAnubhūti?
Seeing fire directly✓ Yes (pratyakṣa)
Inferring fire from smoke✓ Yes (anumāna)
Remembering yesterday's fire✗ No (smṛti)
ŚABDA

Śabda, Śakti, Lakṣaṇā

शब्दः · शक्तिः · लक्षणा

Word, Denotative Power & Secondary Signification
Śaktiईश्वरसंकेतः शक्तिः ।

शक्तिः — the God-ordained (Īśvara-saṃketa) relation between a word and its primary meaning. A word possessing this is śaktaṃ padam. By śakti alone the primary (mukhya) meaning is conveyed.

Lakṣaṇāमुख्यार्थबाधे तत्सम्बन्धे यः अर्थः बोध्यते सा लक्षणा ।

लक्षणा — when the primary meaning is blocked, a related secondary meaning is conveyed. Three kinds:

KindSanskritWhat happensExample
1 जहल्लक्षणा Primary meaning fully abandoned गङ्गायां घोषः — गङ्गा = bank (not stream)
2 अजहल्लक्षणा Primary meaning retained, secondary added Soldier-like qualities implied alongside
3 जहदजहल्लक्षणा Partial retention and partial abandonment तत्त्वमसि (That thou art)

Three functions by which words convey meaning: शक्तिः (primary), लक्षणा (secondary), व्यञ्जना (suggestion).

ŚABDA

Vākya & Śābdabodha

वाक्यम् · शाब्दबोधः

Sentence & Verbal Cognition
Vākya definitionआकाङ्क्षायोग्यतासत्तिमत् पदसमूहः वाक्यम् ।

Three necessary properties of a vākya:

PropertySanskritMeaning
1आकाङ्क्षाSyntactic expectancy — each word expects others to complete the sense
2योग्यताSemantic compatibility — meanings must not contradict each other
3आसत्तिःTemporal proximity — words uttered in close succession

Two kinds of Vākya:

KindSanskritMeaningExample
1विधिवाक्यम्Injunctive — prescribes actionसोमेन यजेत
2निषेधवाक्यम्Prohibitive — forbids actionकलञ्जं न भक्षयेत्

शाब्दबोधः — verbal cognition: knowledge generated when a valid vākya from a reliable speaker (āpta) is understood. Its causes:

CauseRole
शक्तिःPrimary cause — each word's denotative power
आकाङ्क्षाAuxiliary — syntactic expectancy
योग्यताAuxiliary — semantic compatibility
आसत्तिःAuxiliary — temporal proximity
तात्पर्यम्Auxiliary — speaker's intention (resolves ambiguity)

Two kinds of śabda: Vaidika (Vedic, authored by Īśvara — infallible) and Laukika (everyday speech of reliable persons). Both generate valid śābdabodha.
